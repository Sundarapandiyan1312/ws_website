Oven Controlled Crystal Oscillators (OCXOs)

a high-precision frequency source that uses an internal heater (oven) to keep its quartz crystal at a constant temperature, providing exceptional frequency stability and low phase noise, crucial for applications like telecom, military, and test equipment needing accuracy in the parts-per-billion range, far beyond standard oscillators. Morion specializes in these ultra-precise devices, offering various models with features like low G-sensitivity, fast warm-up, and wide frequency ranges.

Temperature Compensated Oscillator (TCXO)

A precision crystal oscillator that uses internal circuitry and a temperature sensor (like a thermistor) to actively correct for frequency shifts caused by temperature changes, providing a highly stable clock signal for demanding applications like telecom, GPS, and IoT, with Morion offering advanced models like the MV202/MV203 providing stability comparable to Oven Controlled Oscillators (OCXOs) but with lower power and faster warm-up.
